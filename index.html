ğŸ§± What Exactly Is a Rubik's Cube?

The standard 3x3 cube has 6 faces, each made up of 9 smaller squares, totaling 54 stickers. The goal? Get all six sides to show just one solid color. Easier said than done!

Inside, it's not just random blocks. The cube contains a clever mechanism allowing rotation in three dimensions, with:

  • 8 corner pieces (each showing 3 colors)
  • 12 edge pieces (each showing 2 colors)
  • 6 fixed center pieces (which don't move and define the face color)

Because of its structure, the Rubik's Cube has exactly 43,252,003,274,489,856,000 possible configurations. That's over 43 quintillion!
