Welcome to the Fast Lane of the Rubik's Cube World!

If you thought solving a Rubik's Cube was impressive, wait until you see someone do it in under 5 seconds. Welcome to speedcubing ‚Äî the competitive sport of solving twisty puzzles as fast as humanly (and sometimes robotically) possible.

üèÅ What Is Speedcubing?

Speedcubing is all about solving puzzles ‚Äî mainly the classic 3x3 cube ‚Äî as quickly as possible using efficient methods, lots of practice, and lightning-fast finger movement (known as finger tricks). Most speedcubers don't rely on luck. Instead, they follow planned solving methods, learn advanced techniques, and train like athletes.

üîß Speedsolving Methods

Here are some popular methods cubers use:

  • CFOP (Fridrich Method) - Most widely used by top cubers. Based on Cross, F2L, OLL, and PLL.
  • Roux Method - A block-building technique used by some world-class solvers.
  • ZZ Method - Less common, but great for smooth turning and fewer rotations.

Most beginners start with the CFOP method‚Äîit's powerful and well-documented.

üß∞ Want to Get Faster?

Whether you're just starting or aiming for sub-20 solves, here are some tips:

  • Practice finger tricks - smoother, faster turning.
  • Time your solves - use tools like cstimer.net or a physical stackmat timer.
  • Upgrade your cube - modern speedcubes (magnetic, smooth-turning) make a huge difference.
  • Learn more algorithms - especially for OLL and PLL.
