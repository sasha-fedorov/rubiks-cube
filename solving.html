How to Solve the Rubik's Cube (Beginner's Guide)

Note: This guide is based on the beginner's method from SolveTheCube.com. All rights and credits for the method and visuals go to them.

üôå Don't Panic‚ÄîSolving the Cube Is Possible!

You might've thought solving a Rubik's Cube is some genius-level trick‚Ä¶ but it's actually a step-by-step process that anyone can learn. No guessing needed‚Äîjust some simple moves, called algorithms, and a bit of practice.

We'll walk you through the beginner method, which focuses on solving the cube layer by layer.

üîß Cube Notation (The Moves)

Before we start, here's how we describe cube moves:

  • F - Front face
  • B - Back face
  • U - Upper face
  • D - Down face
  • L - Left face
  • R - Right face

Each letter simply tells you to move that face clockwise by 90 degrees.

If a letter has ' (a prime) after it, it means a counter-clockwise turn. No ' = clockwise. For example:

  • R = turn right face clockwise
  • R' = turn right face counter-clockwise

Start by making a white ‚Äúplus‚Äù on the white face, aligning each white edge piece with the center of its neighboring face.

‚úÖ Tip: The center pieces don't move‚Äîthey define the face color!

üü® Step 2: Solve the White Corners

Now that you've made a white cross, it's time to finish the whole white face by solving the four white corners.
Your goal is to place each white corner piece between the correct center colors ‚Äî so that the colors on the corner match the sides around it.

üîç What to Do:

Sometimes, a white corner will already be in the bottom layer ‚Äî just not in the right spot or orientation.

Here's how to fix that:

  1. Find a white corner piece in the top layer.
  2. Turn the top face so it's right above where it belongs (for example, the white-blue-red piece goes above the white-blue-red corner).
  3. Then, depending on how the white sticker is facing, use one of these quick moves:

If the white is on the side:

F D F'

If on the right face:

R' D' R

If on the bottom:

R' D R F D2 F'

Repeat this for each corner until your white face is fully solved ‚Äî and the first layer wraps neatly around it!

‚úÖ Tip: If a corner is already in the bottom layer but in the wrong spot or facing the wrong way, no problem. Just use any of the moves above to ‚Äúpop it out‚Äù, then use the right move to place it back correctly.

üü¶ Step 3: Solve the Middle Layer Edges

Now it's time to fill in the middle layer edges (the pieces between the centers, without any yellow on them).

Your goal here is to move edge pieces from the top layer into the middle layer ‚Äî placing them between matching center pieces on each

The top face (usually yellow) should still be scrambled, and we'll now be using it to find and insert the correct edges into the middle.

üß© Step-by-Step:
  1. Look at the top layer for edge pieces that don't have yellow on them.
  2. Once you find one, match its front color with the center piece on that face (by turning the top layer left or right).
  3. Then, check which side the other color needs to go ‚Äî left or right. Use the right move below depending on the direction.

If the edge to go to the left:

U' L' U L U F U' F'

And to go to the right:

U R U' R' U' F' U F

Repeat this process until all four middle layer edges are in the correct positions!

‚úÖ Tip: If a middle edge is in the wrong spot or flipped, just use one of the moves above to pop it out ‚Äî then place it again the right way. Just like you did with the corners!

üü† Step 4: Make a Yellow Cross

Now that the first two layers are done, it's time to work on the yellow face (the top). The goal for this step is to create a yellow cross ‚Äî like the white one you made earlier, but on top!

You don't need to worry about the corner pieces yet ‚Äî just the yellow edge pieces.

üîç What You Might See:

Depending on how your cube looks, you might see one of these:

  • A single yellow dot in the center
  • A small yellow backwards L shape
  • A horizontal yellow line
  • Or maybe you already have a full yellow cross (lucky you!)
üí° The Move to Use:

No matter which shape you start with, you'll use the same short algorithm:

  • F R U R' U' F'

Do this one or more times to turn the yellow dot, L, or line into a full cross.

üîÅ What to Remember:
  • If you see the L shape, hold it so it looks like a backwards L (facing the top-left corner).
  • If you see the line, make sure it's horizontal, not vertical.
  • Repeat the move until all four yellow edge pieces are facing up.

‚úÖ Tip: It might take a couple of tries ‚Äî and that's normal! Just keep using the move above until the yellow cross appears.

üü¢ Step 5: Solve the Last Layer Edges (Side Colors)

Now that you've got a yellow cross, the next goal is to line up the side colors of the yellow edge pieces ‚Äî so they match the center pieces on each face.

The yellow stickers are already facing up, but they might be in the wrong places around the sides.

üîç What to Look For:

Turn the top face to see if any yellow edge piece matches the center color underneath it.

  • If one or more edge pieces match, great!
  • If none of them match, don't worry ‚Äî you'll still use the same move to fix them.
üîÑ The Move to Use:

R U R' U R U2 R' U

This will cycle three edge pieces (all except one in the back) clockwise.

üîÅ What to Do:
  1. Try turning the top layer (U) until at least one edge matches the face color below.
  2. Hold the cube so that solved edge is on the back face.
  3. Do the move above ‚Äî and check again.
  4. You might need to repeat the move once or twice to get all four edge pieces lined up correctly.

‚úÖ Tip: If nothing matches at the start, just do the move from any angle ‚Äî it should fix at least one piece, and then you can try again with that one at the back.

üîÑ Step 6: Position the Yellow Corners Correctly

Now that they're all yellow, we need to move them to their correct locations (without rotating them).
Use:

U R U' L' U R' U' L

Repeat until the corners are in the right places.

üß© Step 7: Finish the Cube (Position Yellow Edges)

Last step! You'll rotate the top edges into the correct positions. Sometimes, you'll need to perform the final algorithm multiple times.
Algorithm:

F2 U L R' F2 L' R U F2
