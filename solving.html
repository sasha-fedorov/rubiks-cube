üîß Cube Notation (The Moves)

Before we start, here's how we describe cube moves:

  • F - Front face
  • B - Back face
  • U - Upper face
  • D - Down face
  • L - Left face
  • R - Right face

Each letter simply tells you to move that face clockwise by 90 degrees.

If a letter has ' (a prime) after it, it means a counter-clockwise turn. No ' = clockwise. For example:

  • R = turn right face clockwise
  • R' = turn right face counter-clockwise

üü® Step 2: Solve the White Corners

Insert the white corner pieces one by one to complete the entire white face (and the first layer).

You'll learn a simple corner algorithm:

Repeat until the white corner is correctly placed.

üü¶ Step 3: Solve the Middle Layer Edges

Now that the white layer is complete, flip the cube so white is on the bottom. Focus on placing the edge pieces in the middle layer.

You'll use one of two algorithms depending on where the piece needs to go (left or right). Don't worry‚Äîthey're easy to memorize with a little practice!

Repeat until the corners are in the right places.
